CDC Statement Regarding Showering with Catheters

The CDC strongly recommends that showering should be permitted if precautions can be taken to reduce the likelihood of introducing organisms into the catheter. (e.g. if the catheter and connection device are protected with an impermeable cover during the shower) and reducing the amount of disconnections of the IV line.

According to CDC statistics, approximately 12% to 25% of BSI (bloodstream infections) associated with IV therapy are fatal. The CDC suggest that these infections can be minimized if proper care is taken to keep the catheter site clean and dry from moisture.
